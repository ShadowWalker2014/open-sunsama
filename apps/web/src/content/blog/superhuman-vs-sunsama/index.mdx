## What is Superhuman?

Superhuman is a premium email client designed for speed. Founded in 2014 and launched to the public in 2017, it became famous for its waitlist, high price point, and near-fanatical user base. The core promise: help you process email twice as fast with half the stress.

### The Superhuman Philosophy

Email dominates modern knowledge work. The average professional spends 28% of their workday on email—more than any other single activity. Superhuman's insight was that if email is inevitable, make it as fast and pleasant as possible.

The result is an email client obsessed with milliseconds. Every interaction is optimized for speed. Screens render instantly. Keyboard shortcuts eliminate mouse usage. AI triages incoming mail before you see it. The goal isn't to reduce email—it's to make email processing feel effortless.

### How Superhuman Works

Superhuman connects to your existing email account (Gmail or Outlook) and provides a new interface built for speed:

1. **Connect your email account**: Link Gmail or Outlook—Superhuman becomes your email interface
2. **Learn keyboard shortcuts**: A built-in tutorial teaches you to navigate without a mouse
3. **Process inbox with Split Inbox**: AI categorizes email into VIP, Important, and Other
4. **Compose with AI assistance**: Superhuman AI helps write and summarize emails
5. **Hit Inbox Zero**: The explicit goal is emptying your inbox regularly

The interface is deliberately minimal—no cluttered sidebars, no visual noise. Everything serves the goal of processing email faster.

### Superhuman Key Features

**Keyboard-First Design**: This defines the Superhuman experience. Every action has a keyboard shortcut. Press "e" to archive, "j/k" to navigate, "c" to compose. Power users never touch their mouse. The learning curve is steep, but the payoff is remarkable email speed.

**Split Inbox**: AI automatically categorizes incoming email into streams: VIP (important senders), Team (colleagues), Important (algorithmic priority), and Other (everything else). Instead of one overwhelming inbox, you process categories in order of importance.

**Superhuman AI**: Built-in AI helps compose emails, summarize long threads, and suggest responses. Write "Reply saying I'll review this next week" and AI generates professional prose. For email-heavy professionals, AI composition saves hours weekly.

**Snippets and Templates**: Save frequently used responses as snippets with variable insertion. Customer success managers, recruiters, and salespeople who send similar emails repeatedly can respond in seconds rather than minutes.

**Scheduled Send and Reminders**: Schedule emails to send later, set reminders to follow up if you don't hear back, and snooze emails to reappear at specific times. These features turn email into a task management system of sorts.

**Read Statuses**: See when recipients open your emails. For sales and outreach, knowing an email was read (but not replied to) changes follow-up strategy.

**Calendar Integration**: View your calendar alongside email to schedule meetings without switching apps. However, this is display-only—Superhuman doesn't manage your calendar.

### Superhuman Pricing in 2026

Superhuman maintains premium positioning:

- **Individual**: $30/month (billed monthly or annually)
- **Team plans**: Custom pricing with admin features
- **No free tier**: Waitlist access only, then full-price subscription

At $360/year for an email client (compared to free Gmail or Outlook), Superhuman requires significant email volume to justify the investment. The target user spends hours daily in email and values the time savings at more than $30/month.

### Superhuman Limitations

**Email Only**: Superhuman is exclusively an email client. It doesn't manage tasks, projects, notes, or calendars. Your email becomes faster, but everything else stays separate.

**No Task Management**: While you can use email as a task system (star important emails, snooze them as reminders), Superhuman doesn't provide true task management with projects, subtasks, or progress tracking.

**No Time Blocking**: You can view your calendar, but you can't create time blocks for focused work. Superhuman helps you process email quickly but doesn't help you plan what to do next.

**No Daily Planning Process**: Opening Superhuman shows your inbox. There's no guidance on what deserves your attention today, no methodology for intentional planning, no rituals for starting or ending your day.

**High Price Point**: At $30/month, Superhuman costs more than many comprehensive productivity suites. The value proposition requires heavy email usage to make economic sense.

**Gmail and Outlook Only**: If your email is on a different provider, Superhuman isn't an option. No support for iCloud, ProtonMail, Fastmail, or self-hosted email.

## What is Sunsama?

Sunsama is a daily planning application built on intentional productivity. While Superhuman asks "how fast can you process email?", Sunsama asks "what actually deserves your time today?" It's designed for knowledge workers who want deliberate daily choices rather than reactive task management.

### The Sunsama Philosophy

Most productivity tools optimize for capturing and completing more tasks. Sunsama takes the opposite approach: help you do less, but do the right things. The core belief is that sustainable productivity comes from intentional daily planning, not faster task processing.

This manifests as a guided daily planning ritual. Each morning, you deliberately choose what matters. You estimate how long tasks will take. You time block your calendar. You confront the reality of limited hours. The methodology builds sustainable habits over time.

### How Sunsama Works

Sunsama's core experience centers on the morning planning ritual:

1. **Review your calendar**: See existing meetings and commitments
2. **Pull in tasks**: Import from connected tools (Todoist, Asana, Notion, Linear, Gmail, and more)
3. **Set daily intentions**: Consciously select what you'll accomplish today
4. **Estimate durations**: Add time estimates to each task
5. **Time block your schedule**: Drag tasks onto calendar slots
6. **Check capacity**: Sunsama warns if you've overcommitted

Throughout the day, you work through time blocks using focus mode. At day's end, an optional shutdown ritual helps reflect and roll incomplete tasks forward.

### Sunsama Key Features

**Guided Daily Planning**: The morning ritual is Sunsama's philosophical foundation. Rather than reacting to whatever demands attention, you proactively choose what deserves your time. The guided process makes intentionality a habit.

**Extensive Integrations**: Sunsama connects to 30+ productivity tools: Todoist, Trello, Asana, Notion, ClickUp, Jira, Linear, GitHub, Gmail, Slack, Outlook, and more. Tasks flow from source systems into your daily plan. Work where tasks originate, plan in Sunsama.

**Email-to-Task Conversion**: Pull emails from Gmail or Outlook into your task list. An important email becomes a task with context, estimated duration, and scheduled time—rather than something buried in your inbox hoping to be remembered.

**Two-Way Calendar Sync**: Full bidirectional sync with Google Calendar, Outlook, and iCloud. Time blocks you create in Sunsama appear on your external calendar. Your calendar becomes an execution plan, not just a meeting schedule.

**Focus Mode**: When working on a task, focus mode expands it to fill your screen with a timer running. Pomodoro support structures deep work sessions. This execution support separates Sunsama from email-only tools.

**Overplanning Prevention**: Sunsama tracks estimated durations and warns when you've scheduled more than realistic. This promotes sustainable productivity over burnout-inducing overcommitment.

**Weekly Review**: Beyond daily planning, Sunsama provides weekly review features to assess progress, understand time allocation, and plan strategically.

## Email-Centric vs Calendar-Centric Productivity

The Superhuman vs Sunsama choice often reflects how you think about productivity itself.

### The Email-Centric View (Superhuman)

Some professionals operate primarily through email:

- **Sales and business development**: Outreach, follow-ups, and deal communication happen in email
- **Customer success**: Client communication is email-heavy
- **Executives**: Decision-making happens through email threads and approvals
- **Consultants**: Client work coordination flows through email

For these users, email IS the work. Processing email faster directly increases productivity. An empty inbox isn't just satisfaction—it's completion of real work. Superhuman's promise of 2x email speed translates to meaningful time savings.

### The Calendar-Centric View (Sunsama)

Other professionals think in terms of calendar blocks and tasks:

- **Developers and engineers**: Work happens in focused blocks, not email threads
- **Writers and creatives**: Deep work requires protected time, not faster email
- **Project managers**: Work flows through tasks, not primarily through email
- **Individual contributors**: Output comes from focused execution, not communication volume

For these users, email is overhead—necessary but not the actual work. Faster email processing doesn't increase output; intentional time allocation does. Sunsama's planning methodology addresses the real productivity lever.

### The Hybrid Reality

Most knowledge workers need both—some email handling AND intentional planning. The question is which tool serves as your productivity hub:

- **Email hub with planning add-ons**: Use Superhuman for email, and another tool for task management. Email is primary; planning supplements it.
- **Planning hub with email integration**: Use Sunsama for daily planning, pull emails in as tasks, but handle actual email in a standard client. Planning is primary; email feeds into it.

Neither approach is wrong. The choice depends on where your work primarily happens.
