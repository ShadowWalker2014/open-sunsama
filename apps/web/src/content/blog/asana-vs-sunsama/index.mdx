## What is Asana?

Asana is one of the world's most popular project management platforms, founded in 2008 by Facebook co-founder Dustin Moskovitz and engineer Justin Rosenstein. With over 150,000 paying organizations and millions of free users, Asana has become the go-to tool for teams coordinating work across projects, departments, and timelines.

### How Asana Works

Asana organizes work into a hierarchy: **Workspaces** contain **Projects**, which contain **Tasks** (and optionally **Subtasks**). Teams can view work in multiple formats—List, Board (Kanban), Timeline (Gantt), and Calendar views—depending on what suits their workflow.

The platform excels at:

- **Project templates**: Start new initiatives quickly with pre-built structures
- **Task dependencies**: Link tasks so teams know what to work on next
- **Custom fields**: Track metadata like priority, status, or cost
- **Workload management**: See team capacity and redistribute work
- **Goals and portfolios**: Align projects to company objectives

### Asana's Key Features

**Multiple Project Views**: Switch between List, Board, Timeline, and Calendar views to see your work from different angles. Timeline view is particularly powerful for understanding project schedules and dependencies.

**Team Collaboration**: Comment on tasks, @mention teammates, attach files, and use approval workflows. Asana is built for teams to work together, not individuals planning their day.

**Rules and Automation**: Create automations to route tasks, update fields, or notify teammates when conditions are met. Premium users can build complex workflows without code.

**Reporting and Dashboards**: Build custom reports to track progress across projects. Useful for managers monitoring team output and project status.

**Extensive Integrations**: Over 200 integrations including Slack, Microsoft Teams, Google Workspace, Salesforce, Jira, and more. Asana aims to be the central hub for all work coordination.

### Asana Pricing in 2026

Asana offers a functional free tier and several paid plans:

**Personal (Free)** includes:
- Unlimited tasks, projects, and storage
- Basic List, Board, and Calendar views

**Starter**: $10.99/user/month (billed annually, ~$132/user/year)

For individuals, Asana's free tier provides solid task management. But notice what's missing: **no time blocking, no daily planning ritual, no focus mode**. Asana helps you organize work—it doesn't help you decide what to work on today.

### Asana's Limitations for Daily Planning

**No Time Blocking**: Asana shows your tasks alongside calendar events, but you can't drag tasks onto specific time slots. There's no concept of "I'll work on this from 9-11am." Asana daily planning simply isn't the tool's focus.

**No Planning Ritual**: Each day starts with your task list—unchanged from yesterday. There's no guided process to review your calendar, assess your energy, and intentionally choose today's focus.

**Team-Centric Design**: Every feature assumes collaboration. Even personal projects feel like you're using a tool designed for someone else. The overhead of projects, sections, and custom fields is overkill for individual task management.

**Overwhelming for Personal Use**: Asana's power comes from features that individuals rarely need. Portfolios, goals, workload views, and advanced reporting are essential for teams—but noise for someone just trying to plan their day.

## What is Sunsama?

Sunsama is a daily planner built on the philosophy of calm, intentional productivity. Rather than helping you manage all your work, Sunsama focuses on one question: **what will you accomplish today?**

### How Sunsama Works

Sunsama centers on the daily planning ritual. Each morning, the app guides you through a structured process:

1. **Review your calendar**: See meetings and commitments that already claim your time
2. **Pull in tasks**: Import tasks from Asana, Trello, Notion, Todoist, and other tools
3. **Set time estimates**: Decide how long each task will realistically take
4. **Time block your day**: Drag tasks onto specific calendar slots
5. **Check for overplanning**: Sunsama warns if you've scheduled more than is achievable

Throughout the day, you work through your time blocks. At day's end, an optional shutdown ritual helps you reflect and roll incomplete tasks to tomorrow.

### Sunsama's Key Features

**Guided Daily Planning**: The morning ritual isn't just a feature—it's Sunsama's core philosophy. Planning becomes a mindfulness practice, not administrative overhead.

**Deep Integrations with Work Tools**: Sunsama shines at pulling tasks from wherever they originate. Native integrations include Asana, Todoist, Trello, Notion, ClickUp, Jira, Linear, GitHub, Gmail, and Slack. Two-way sync means completing a task in Sunsama marks it done in Asana.

**Calendar-First Design**: Everything revolves around your calendar. Tasks without scheduled times are second-class citizens. This forces intentionality—if it doesn't have time on your calendar, does it really deserve your attention today?

**[Focus Mode](/features/focus-mode)**: When you start a task, Sunsama's focus mode expands it to fill your screen, starts a timer, and minimizes distractions. Includes Pomodoro support for structured work sessions.

**Overplanning Prevention**: Sunsama tracks estimated durations and warns when you've scheduled more than realistically fits in a day. This sustainable productivity approach helps prevent burnout.

### Sunsama Pricing in 2026

Sunsama is premium-priced for knowledge workers:

- **Monthly**: $25/month
- **Annual**: $20/month (billed as $240/year)
- **14-day free trial** (no credit card required)

There is no free tier. Sunsama's pricing reflects its positioning as a professional tool for people who value their time.

### Sunsama's Limitations

**Expensive**: At $240/year, Sunsama costs significantly more than most productivity tools. For freelancers, students, or cost-conscious professionals, this is a real barrier.

**Individual-Only**: No team features whatsoever. You can't share tasks, collaborate on projects, or see teammates' schedules. Sunsama is purely personal.

**Limited Task Management**: Sunsama expects you to manage tasks elsewhere (in Asana, Notion, etc.) and pull them in daily. There's no robust project structure, subtask management, or organization features.

**Mobile is Secondary**: While Sunsama has iOS and Android apps, the experience is optimized for desktop. Mobile users may find the apps limiting.

**Sunsama**: Time blocking is the core experience. Every task should have a calendar slot. Drag-and-drop scheduling is smooth, and blocks sync bidirectionally with Google Calendar and Outlook. Learn more about [what time blocking is](/blog/what-is-time-blocking).

**Verdict**: Sunsama wins decisively—Asana doesn't even compete in this category.

### Task Management

**Asana**: This is Asana's strength. Projects, sections, subtasks, dependencies, custom fields, templates, and powerful filtering make Asana excellent for managing complex work.

**Sunsama**: Basic task management only. Create tasks, add descriptions, set estimates. No projects, limited subtasks, minimal organization. Sunsama expects tasks to live elsewhere.

**Verdict**: Asana dominates task management. Sunsama isn't trying to compete here.

### Team Collaboration

**Asana**: Built for teams from the ground up. Comments, @mentions, task assignments, file sharing, approval workflows, and shared projects make collaboration seamless.

**Sunsama**: No collaboration features. Personal productivity only.

**Verdict**: Asana wins by default—Sunsama is individual-only by design.

### Calendar Integration

**Asana**: One-way calendar display. Your Google Calendar events appear in Asana's calendar view, but Asana tasks don't write back to your calendar. No true integration.

**Sunsama**: Full two-way sync with Google Calendar, Outlook, and iCloud. Time-blocked tasks appear on your external calendar. Calendar events inform daily planning.

**Verdict**: Sunsama's calendar integration is far more powerful.

### Daily Planning Workflow

**Asana**: No planning ritual. You start each day staring at your project lists, unchanged from yesterday. No guidance on what to prioritize or how much is realistic.

**Sunsama**: Structured daily planning is the core product. Morning ritual guides you through reviewing commitments, selecting tasks, and creating a realistic schedule.

**Verdict**: Sunsama is designed for daily planning; Asana isn't.

### Focus Features

**Asana**: No focus mode, no timers, no distraction-free environment. Asana is about coordination, not concentration.

**Sunsama**: Full focus mode with task expansion, timers, and Pomodoro support. Designed to help you execute, not just plan.

**Verdict**: Sunsama wins—Asana offers nothing here.

### Integrations

**Asana**: 200+ integrations across categories. Connects with almost any tool your team uses.

**Sunsama**: 15+ focused integrations with popular productivity tools. Quality over quantity, with deep two-way sync.

**Verdict**: Asana has more integrations, but Sunsama's integrations are deeper for individual productivity.

## When to Use Asana

Asana is the right choice if you:

**Best for**: Teams, project managers, organizations coordinating complex work across departments.

**Not for**: Individuals planning their personal daily schedule. Asana has no time blocking, no planning ritual, and overwhelming complexity for personal use.

- **Use multiple productivity tools**: You have tasks in Asana, emails in Gmail, and items in Notion—and need to consolidate them for daily planning
- **Live by your calendar**: Time blocking is central to how you work
- **Want to prevent overcommitment**: You appreciate warnings when you've scheduled more than is realistic
- **Can justify $240/year**: The price is reasonable for a tool you'll use every day

**Best for**: Knowledge workers, consultants, professionals with fragmented task sources who want calm, intentional daily planning.

**Not for**: Teams needing collaboration, budget-conscious users, or anyone who wants comprehensive task management in one place.
