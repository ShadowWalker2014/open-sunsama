## What is ClickUp?

ClickUp is one of the most ambitious productivity platforms ever built. Founded in 2017, it has grown explosively to over 800,000 teams worldwide by promising to replace "all your work apps." The tagline is bold: one app for tasks, docs, goals, chat, whiteboards, and more.

### How ClickUp Works

ClickUp organizes work in a hierarchical structure: **Workspace** → **Spaces** → **Folders** → **Lists** → **Tasks**. This gives teams incredible flexibility to organize complex projects—but the depth can overwhelm individuals just trying to plan their day.

The platform offers multiple ways to view your work:

- **List View**: Traditional task list with custom columns
- **Board View**: Kanban-style workflow visualization
- **Calendar View**: Tasks displayed on a calendar
- **Timeline View**: Gantt-style project timelines
- **Table View**: Spreadsheet-like data management
- **Workload View**: Team capacity planning
- **Whiteboard**: Visual brainstorming
- **Docs**: Built-in documentation

### ClickUp's Key Features

**Everything App Approach**: ClickUp's strength—and weakness—is its breadth. You can manage projects, write docs, track time, set goals, create automations, build dashboards, chat with teammates, and more. All in one platform.

**Powerful Customization**: Custom fields, custom statuses, task types, automations, and dashboards let teams build workflows tailored to their processes. The flexibility is nearly unlimited.

**Templates Galore**: Thousands of templates for project management, marketing, software development, and more. Starting from a template beats building from scratch.

**Time Tracking**: Built-in time tracking for tasks lets you see how long work actually takes versus estimates. Useful for agencies and consultants who bill hourly.

**Integrations**: Over 1,000 integrations connect ClickUp with virtually any tool—Slack, Google Workspace, Outlook, Zoom, GitHub, Figma, and many more.

For individuals, ClickUp's free tier is remarkably capable. But here's what's missing for daily planning: **no guided planning ritual, basic time blocking support, and overwhelming complexity** when all you need is to schedule your day.

### ClickUp's Limitations for Daily Planning

**Feature Overload**: ClickUp's power becomes noise when you just want to plan your day. Spaces, folders, lists, custom fields, automations—the options are endless, but most are irrelevant for personal daily planning.

**Limited Time Blocking**: While ClickUp has calendar view, ClickUp time blocking isn't a first-class feature. You can't drag tasks directly onto time slots the way dedicated daily planners allow. Tasks have start dates and due dates, but not "I'll work on this from 2-4pm today."

**No Planning Ritual**: Each day, ClickUp presents your task lists unchanged from yesterday. There's no guided process to review your calendar, estimate today's work, and create an intentional schedule. ClickUp daily planning requires you to build your own workflow.

**Steep Learning Curve**: ClickUp has so many features that new users report weeks of setup and learning. If you just want to plan tomorrow, that's too much investment.

**Analysis Paralysis**: With infinite customization comes decision fatigue. Should you use Spaces or Folders? Custom statuses or tags? Automations or manual workflows? The options are overwhelming for simple daily planning.

## What is Sunsama?

Sunsama is the anti-ClickUp. While ClickUp tries to do everything, Sunsama does one thing exceptionally well: **help you plan and execute your day with intention**.

Founded on principles of calm productivity, Sunsama rejects the hustle culture that celebrates 60-hour work weeks. Instead, it asks: what's actually achievable today? What deserves your time and attention?

### How Sunsama Works

Sunsama centers on the **daily planning ritual**—a structured process you complete each morning:

1. **Review your calendar**: See meetings and commitments that already claim your time
2. **Pull in tasks**: Import tasks from ClickUp, Asana, Todoist, Notion, Linear, and other tools
3. **Set time estimates**: Decide how long each task will realistically take
4. **Time block your day**: Drag tasks onto specific calendar slots
5. **Check for overplanning**: Sunsama warns if you've scheduled more than is achievable

The morning ritual isn't just a feature—it's Sunsama's core philosophy. Planning becomes a mindfulness practice that starts each day with clarity.

### Sunsama's Key Features

**Guided Daily Planning**: The structured morning ritual transforms chaotic task lists into an intentional daily schedule. Sunsama doesn't just show you tasks—it guides you through deciding what matters today.

**Calendar-First Design**: Everything revolves around your calendar. Tasks without scheduled times are second-class citizens. If it doesn't have time on your calendar, does it really deserve your attention today?

**Deep Task Integrations**: Sunsama shines at pulling tasks from wherever they originate. Native integrations include ClickUp, Asana, Todoist, Trello, Notion, Jira, Linear, GitHub, Gmail, and Slack. Two-way sync means completing a task in Sunsama marks it done in ClickUp.

**[Focus Mode](/features/focus-mode)**: Start a task and Sunsama expands it full-screen, starts a timer, and minimizes distractions. Pomodoro support helps structure work sessions for sustained concentration.

**Overplanning Prevention**: Sunsama tracks estimated durations and warns when you've scheduled more than realistically fits in a day. This sustainable approach helps prevent burnout from chronic overcommitment.

**Shutdown Ritual**: At day's end, an optional reflection helps you review what was accomplished and roll incomplete tasks to tomorrow. Each day gets a clean start.

**Limited Task Management**: Sunsama expects tasks to live elsewhere—in ClickUp, Notion, or other tools—and be pulled in for daily planning. There's no robust project structure, minimal subtasks, and basic organization.

**No Documentation**: Unlike ClickUp's docs feature, Sunsama is purely task-focused. Notes live in other tools.

**Desktop-Optimized**: While Sunsama has mobile apps, the experience is optimized for desktop. The daily planning ritual works best on a larger screen.

## ClickUp vs Sunsama: Feature-by-Feature Comparison

Let's compare these tools across the features that matter for daily productivity.

### Time Blocking

**ClickUp**: Has calendar view and time tracking, but ClickUp [time blocking](/features/time-blocking) isn't intuitive. Tasks have start dates and due dates, not dedicated time blocks. You can create calendar events manually, but there's no drag-and-drop from task list to calendar. For serious time blocking, ClickUp requires workarounds.

**Sunsama**: Time blocking is the core experience. Drag tasks directly onto your calendar to schedule specific work times. Blocks sync bidirectionally with Google Calendar and Outlook, so colleagues see when you're busy. Learn more about [what time blocking is](/blog/what-is-time-blocking).

**Verdict**: Sunsama wins decisively. Time blocking is its primary feature; in ClickUp, it's an afterthought.

### Task Management

**ClickUp**: This is ClickUp's home turf. Multiple views, subtasks, dependencies, custom fields, automations, recurring tasks, task templates—ClickUp handles complex task management at any scale.

**Sunsama**: Basic task management only. Create tasks, add notes, set estimates. Limited subtasks, no dependencies, minimal organization. Sunsama expects comprehensive task management to happen elsewhere.

**Verdict**: ClickUp dominates task management. Sunsama isn't trying to compete here.

### Project Management

**ClickUp**: Full project management with Gantt timelines, milestones, portfolios, and goals. Manage complex multi-month initiatives with dependencies and team assignments.

**Sunsama**: No project management. Sunsama focuses on today, not multi-week project plans. Pull project tasks from other tools for daily scheduling.

**Verdict**: ClickUp by default—Sunsama doesn't have project management.

### Team Collaboration

**ClickUp**: Built for teams with comments, @mentions, real-time editing, shared views, permissions, and team chat. Multiple people can work in the same workspace simultaneously.

**Sunsama**: No collaboration features. Personal productivity only.

**Verdict**: ClickUp wins by default—Sunsama is individual-only by design.

### Documentation

**ClickUp**: ClickUp Docs provides a built-in wiki and documentation system. Create docs, embed tasks, collaborate in real-time. Decent alternative to Notion for internal documentation.

**Sunsama**: No documentation features. Notes are task-level only.

**Verdict**: ClickUp—Sunsama has nothing comparable.

### Calendar Integration

**ClickUp**: Full two-way sync with Google Calendar and Outlook. Calendar events appear in ClickUp's calendar view, and ClickUp tasks can create calendar events. Solid integration.

**Sunsama**: Deep calendar integration is core to the product. Two-way sync with Google Calendar, Outlook, and iCloud. Calendar events inform your daily plan and time blocks appear on external calendars.

**Verdict**: Both offer good calendar integration, but Sunsama's calendar-first design makes it feel more native.

### Daily Planning Workflow

**ClickUp**: No planning ritual. Your task views look the same every morning. Creating a daily plan requires manually filtering tasks by due date and checking your calendar separately. ClickUp daily planning is DIY.

**Sunsama**: Structured daily planning is the entire product. The guided morning ritual walks you through reviewing commitments, pulling in tasks, setting estimates, and creating a realistic schedule.

**Verdict**: Sunsama is designed for daily planning; ClickUp isn't.

### Focus Features

**ClickUp**: Basic time tracking and a "Focus Mode" that reduces sidebar distractions. No built-in timer, no Pomodoro, no deep focus support.

**Sunsama**: Full focus mode with task expansion, countdown timer, and Pomodoro support. Designed to help you execute, not just plan.

**Verdict**: Sunsama's focus features are more developed.

### Integrations

**ClickUp**: Over 1,000 integrations. If a productivity tool exists, ClickUp probably connects to it. This is a major advantage for teams using many tools.

**Sunsama**: 15+ focused integrations with popular productivity tools. Quality over quantity—integrations are deep with two-way task sync.

**Verdict**: ClickUp has more integrations; Sunsama's are deeper for daily planning workflows.

### Learning Curve

**ClickUp**: Notoriously steep. New users report spending weeks configuring workspaces before feeling productive. The flexibility creates setup overhead.

**Sunsama**: Gentle onboarding. The daily planning ritual is intuitive, and there's little to configure. Start planning within minutes.

**Verdict**: Sunsama is significantly easier to learn.
