## What is Calendly?

Calendly is a meeting scheduling platform that eliminates the back-and-forth of finding meeting times. Instead of exchanging emails asking "Does Tuesday at 3pm work?", you share a Calendly link that shows your availability, and others book directly onto your calendar.

### How Calendly Works

The core Calendly workflow is straightforward:

1. **Connect your calendar**: Link Google Calendar, Outlook, or iCloud
2. **Set your availability**: Define when you're open for meetings
3. **Create event types**: Configure different meeting durations and purposes
4. **Share your link**: Send your scheduling page to anyone who needs to book time
5. **Automatic booking**: Invitees see your availability and book without your involvement

When someone books, Calendly creates the calendar event, sends confirmations, and can trigger integrations like video conferencing links, CRM updates, or payment collection.

### Calendly's Key Features

**Scheduling Links**: The core value proposition. Share a link; people book meetings. No email chains required.

**Availability Management**: Set working hours, buffer time between meetings, minimum notice periods, and maximum meetings per day. Your availability updates in real-time based on existing calendar events.

**Multiple Event Types**: Create different booking options—15-minute calls, 30-minute demos, 60-minute consultations—each with its own availability rules.

**Team Scheduling**: Round-robin distribution for sales teams, collective scheduling for group meetings, and shared scheduling pages for departments.

**Integrations**: Native connections with Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, Salesforce, HubSpot, Stripe, PayPal, and hundreds of other tools via Zapier.

**Automated Workflows**: Send confirmation emails, reminder sequences, and follow-up messages automatically.

### Calendly Pricing in 2026

Calendly offers tiered pricing:

- **Free**: One active event type, basic integrations
- **Standard**: $12/month—unlimited event types, integrations, automated workflows
- **Teams**: $20/month/user—team scheduling, round-robin, admin controls
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing—SSO, advanced security, dedicated support

For individual users, the free tier handles basic scheduling. Power users typically need Standard ($12/month) for unlimited event types and integrations.

## What is Sunsama?

Sunsama is a daily planning application built on the principle of intentional, sustainable productivity. Rather than letting meetings and tasks accumulate chaotically, Sunsama guides you through deliberately planning each day—choosing what deserves your time and scheduling when you'll do it.

### How Sunsama Works

Sunsama's core experience is the daily planning ritual:

1. **Morning review**: See your calendar commitments for the day
2. **Import tasks**: Pull tasks from connected tools (Todoist, Asana, Notion, Jira, Linear, Trello)
3. **Set daily intentions**: Consciously decide what you'll accomplish
4. **Estimate time**: Add duration estimates to each task
5. **Time block**: Drag tasks onto specific calendar slots
6. **Check capacity**: Sunsama warns if you've overcommitted

Throughout the day, focus mode helps you execute tasks with Pomodoro timers and distraction reduction. An evening shutdown ritual helps you reflect and roll incomplete work forward.

### Sunsama's Key Features

**Guided Daily Planning**: Structured morning ritual that shapes how you approach your workday. You make deliberate choices rather than reacting to whatever seems urgent.

**Task Management**: Comprehensive [task management](/blog/what-is-task-management) with subtasks, notes, time estimates, and project organization. Unlike minimal to-do apps, Sunsama treats tasks as first-class entities.

**Time Blocking**: Drag tasks onto your calendar to schedule specific work periods. [Time blocking](/features/time-blocking) keeps tasks and calendar events connected—update one, and the other reflects the change.

**Extensive Integrations**: Native connections with Todoist, Trello, Asana, Notion, ClickUp, Jira, Linear, GitHub, Gmail, Slack, and more. Tasks flow from source systems into your daily plan.

**Two-Way Calendar Sync**: Full bidirectional sync with Google Calendar, Outlook, and iCloud. Time blocks appear on your external calendar; external events show in Sunsama.

**Focus Mode**: Expand a task to full screen with a running timer. [Focus mode](/features/focus-mode) with Pomodoro technique support structures deep work sessions.

## The Core Difference: Scheduling vs Planning

Understanding Calendly vs Sunsama requires recognizing they address completely different problems.

### What Calendly Solves

Calendly answers: "How do I let others book time with me efficiently?"

The problem it solves is coordination friction. When you need to meet with clients, candidates, prospects, or colleagues, finding mutually available times traditionally requires multiple emails. Calendly eliminates this by showing your availability and letting others self-schedule.

Calendly is about **external coordination**—managing how other people access your time.

### What Sunsama Solves

Sunsama answers: "How do I spend my time intentionally each day?"

The problem it solves is planning chaos. When you have tasks in multiple systems, meetings scattered across your calendar, and more to do than time allows, intentional planning becomes difficult. Sunsama provides structure for deciding what matters and scheduling when you'll do it.

Sunsama is about **internal planning**—managing how you use your own time.

### The Overlap (and Lack Thereof)

These tools share one thing: calendar integration. Both connect to your Google Calendar, Outlook, or iCloud calendar. But they use that connection differently:

- **Calendly** reads your calendar to determine availability for external bookings
- **Sunsama** reads and writes to your calendar as part of daily time blocking

Calendly doesn't care what tasks you need to accomplish. Sunsama doesn't care how meetings get scheduled. They operate in adjacent but separate domains.

In this workflow:

They don't compete; they complement.

## When Calendly Is Enough

Calendly alone serves many users perfectly. It's likely sufficient if:

**Your role is meeting-heavy**: Sales representatives, recruiters, consultants, and client-facing roles often spend most of their time in meetings. If meetings are your primary work rather than something between focused work sessions, scheduling optimization matters more than task planning.

**You don't struggle with daily planning**: Some people naturally know what to work on each day without structured guidance. If you manage your tasks effectively in your head or with minimal tooling, Sunsama's planning methodology isn't necessary.

**Task management happens elsewhere**: If you already use Asana, Notion, Linear, or another tool for task management and it works well, you might not need Sunsama's task features.

**Budget is constrained**: Calendly's free tier covers basic scheduling. If you can't justify $240/year for daily planning software, Calendly alone might be the pragmatic choice.

**You work reactively by design**: Some roles require responding to inbound requests rather than planning proactively. Customer support, on-call engineers, and similar roles may find Calendly valuable while finding daily planning rituals impractical.

## When You Need Sunsama-Style Planning

Daily planning tools become valuable when:

**Tasks and calendar are disconnected**: You maintain separate to-do lists and calendars with no integration. What you need to do and when you'll do it exist in separate mental spaces.

**You want to time block but find it tedious**: Manually creating calendar events for each work session isn't sustainable. You need tasks and calendar to be connected.

**Daily planning feels chaotic**: You start each morning unsure what to prioritize. Too often, you end days feeling busy but unproductive.

**Focus is difficult**: Concentration is challenging, and you'd benefit from timers and dedicated focus modes to structure deep work.

**Tasks come from multiple sources**: Your work arrives via email, Slack, Jira, Asana, and various other channels. You need unified planning across sources.

**You consistently overcommit**: You schedule more than you can accomplish and need help planning realistically.

If multiple items resonate, Sunsama-style planning addresses a real need—but the $240/year price tag remains significant.
